Normal Tissue Tolerance

Homework Assignment

1. PURPOSE: Why This Matters

  • Knowledge You Will Gain: This lesson introduces one of the most important concepts in treatment planning: normal tissue tolerance. You will learn that every organ has a radiation dose limit (TD 5/5) and the potential consequences (endpoints) of exceeding that limit.
  • Skills You Will Practice: You will use an interactive diagram to learn key organ tolerances. Then, you will practice the critical clinical skill of plan evaluation by analyzing a Dose Volume Histogram (DVH) to determine if a treatment plan is safe.
  • Relevance to Your Profession: Your primary job as a radiation therapist is to deliver treatment safely. This means understanding not just the dose to the tumor, but also the dose to surrounding healthy organs. Recognizing the significance of a DVH and knowing critical organ dose limits is essential for daily quality assurance and for understanding the clinical rationale behind every treatment plan.

Self-Check: Answers & Key Concepts

Part 1: Data Gathering

  • Spinal Cord: TD 5/5 is ~47 Gy. The endpoint is Myelitis/Paralysis.
  • Heart: TD 5/5 for the whole organ is ~40 Gy. The endpoint is Pericarditis.
  • Lung (Total): TD 5/5 for both lungs is ~17.5 Gy (mean dose). The endpoint is Pneumonitis.

Part 2: DVH Analysis

  • Spinal Cord Safety: Yes, the plan is safe. The max dose of 45 Gy is below the ~47 Gy tolerance limit.
  • Heart Safety: Yes, the plan is safe. The mean dose of 25 Gy is below the ~40 Gy tolerance limit.
  • Lung Safety: Yes, the plan is safe. The mean dose of 15 Gy is below the ~17.5 Gy tolerance limit. This indicates the patient has a low risk (<5%) of developing severe radiation pneumonitis from this plan.
