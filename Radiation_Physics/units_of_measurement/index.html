The Hierarchy of SI Units in Radiation Science

From basic building blocks to specialized clinical units.

1. Basic & Derived SI Units

All physical measurements are built upon seven Basic SI Units. In our field, the most critical are the meter (m), kilogram (kg), and second (s). When we combine these, we get Derived Units, such as velocity (m/s) or energy (Joule). These are the true foundation of all our other measurements.

2. The Bridge: From Derived to Special Units

For convenience, some complex derived units are given special names. This is how our key radiologic units are born.

  • Absorbed Dose: The derived unit is energy per mass (Joule/kilogram). We give this the special name Gray (Gy).
  • Radioactivity: The derived unit is decays per time (1/second). We give this the special name Becquerel (Bq).
  • Equivalent Dose: This is also based on J/kg, but includes a biological weighting factor. We give this the special name Sievert (Sv).
  • Exposure/Ionization in Air: Measures ionization in air, with the derived unit Coulomb/kilogram.

3. Practical Use: Radiologic Units & Prefixes

While the Gray, Becquerel, and Sievert are the official SI units, we use metric prefixes to handle the vast range of values seen in the clinic.
