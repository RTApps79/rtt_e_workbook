Basics of Radiation Therapy Planning

This lesson covers the fundamental steps and concepts involved in planning and delivering radiation therapy.

Aim of Radiotherapy

The primary goal of radiotherapy is to deliver a tumoricidal dose to a defined target volume while minimizing damage to surrounding healthy tissues, aiming for an optimum therapeutic ratio.

Steps in Planning

  • Preplanning: Includes evaluating and staging the tumor.
  • Planning Radiotherapy Treatment: Involves describing the treatment and immobilizing the patient.
  • Treatment Delivery: Encompasses dose prescription and monitoring for morbidity.

Steps in Preplanning

  • Clinical Evaluation: History and clinical examination, confirmation of malignancy, etc.
  • Intent of Treatment: Radical or palliative approaches.
  • Choice of Treatment: Tumor board decision, modality sequences, etc.

Planning Radiotherapy

Detailed phase includes patient positioning, tumor localization, and defining target volumes.

Treatment Delivery

Final stage includes dose prescription, implementation, verification, and monitoring.
