Shielding Calculation Factors

When designing a vault, how thick must the walls be? This depends on three factors: W, U, and T.

2.1 The Barrier Formula Variables

Formula: Barrier Thickness ∝ (W × U × T)

Factor Name Definition
W Workload How much is the machine on?
Unit: cGy/week or mA-min/week.
U Use Factor How often is the beam pointed at this specific wall?
Floors: U = 1 (Beam points down often).
Walls: U = 0.25 (Beam rotates).
T Occupancy Factor Who is on the other side of the wall?
T=1: Offices, Control Rooms (Full occupancy).
T=1/5: Hallways, Corridors (Partial).
T=1/20: Toilets, Parking Lots (Occasional).

Protons vs. Photons

3.1 The Bragg Peak

Why use Protons? Because they stop.

  • Photons (X-rays): Have an exit dose (pass through the patient).
  • Protons: Deposit most energy at a specific depth (Bragg Peak) and then stop immediately. Zero exit dose. Ideal for Pediatric tumors and Spine.

Chemotherapy & Pharmacology

Drugs that interact with radiation.

4.1 Radiosensitizers vs. Radioprotectors

Class Drug Example Function
Radiosensitizer Doxorubicin (Adriamycin) Increases the effect of radiation on the tumor (but also increases toxicity).
Warning: Doxorubicin is cardiotoxic (heart damage).
Radioprotector Amifostine (Ethyol) Protects normal tissue (specifically salivary glands) from radiation damage.

4.2 Specific Toxicities

Memorize these drug-organ associations:

  • Bleomycin → Lung Toxicity (Pulmonary Fibrosis).
  • Adriamycin (Doxorubicin) → Heart Toxicity (Cardiotoxicity).
  • Vincristine → Neurotoxicity (Nerve damage).
  • Cisplatin → Kidney & Hearing Toxicity (Nephro/Ototoxicity).
