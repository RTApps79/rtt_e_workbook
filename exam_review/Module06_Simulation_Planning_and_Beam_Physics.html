ICRU 50 & 62 Definitions

You must know the "nesting doll" order of these volumes.

1.1 The Volume Hierarchy

Volume Definition Purpose
GTV (Gross Tumor Volume) Palpable or visible extent of tumor. What you can see on the scan.
CTV (Clinical Target Volume) GTV + Microscopic disease. Covers invisible spread. (e.g., margins around a lumpectomy).
PTV (Planning Target Volume) CTV + Setup Margins. Accounts for patient movement and setup error.
Treated Volume Volume enclosed by the prescribed isodose line (e.g., the 95% line). Usually larger than PTV.
Irradiated Volume Volume receiving significant dose (usually >50%). Includes normal tissue.
2

CT Simulation Physics

2.1 Hounsfield Units (CT Numbers)

CT scanners measure density (attenuation) and assign a number. The Treatment Planning System (TPS) needs this to calculate dose absorption.

Material Hounsfield Unit (HU)
Dense Bone +1000
Muscle / Tissue +10 to +50
Water 0 (Baseline)
Fat -50 to -100
Air -1000
Key Concept: If you treat through a hip replacement (Metal), the HU is extremely high (streak artifact). The TPS might miscalculate dose unless you "override" the density.

2.2 Contrast Media

  • Barium Sulfate: Used for GI tract. Not water soluble. Do NOT use if perforation is suspected (use Gastrografin).
  • Iodinated Contrast (IV): Used to visualize vessels/organs.
    • Ionic: High osmolality (higher risk of reaction).
    • Non-Ionic: Low osmolality (safer, less toxic).
3

Photon Beam Characteristics

3.1 The PDD Curve & Dmax

Photons penetrate through the body. Dmax is the depth of electronic equilibrium (100% dose).

Energy Dmax Depth Surface Dose
6 MV 1.5 cm ~30-40%
10 MV 2.5 cm ~25%
15 MV 3.0 cm ~15%
18 MV 3.5 cm ~15%
Skin Sparing: Higher Energy = Deeper Dmax = Lower Skin Dose (Better Sparing).
4

Electron Beam Characteristics

Electrons behave differently than photons. They have a finite range and are used for superficial tumors.

4.1 Electron Isodose Distribution

Unlike the penetrating photon beam, electrons balloon out and stop quickly.

  • Ballooning: Low isodose lines (10-20%) bulge OUT laterally at depth.
  • Constriction: High isodose lines (90%) pull IN (taper).
  • Surface Dose: Electrons have a HIGH surface dose (80-90%) compared to photons. Skin sparing is minimal.

4.2 Rules of Thumb (Memorize These)

You must be able to select the correct energy for a tumor depth.

Rule Formula Use Case
Therapeutic Range (90% Line) E / 4 "The tumor is 3cm deep. What energy do you use?"
3cm x 4 = 12 MeV needed.
80% Isodose Line E / 3 Often used as the standard prescription line.
Practical Range (Rp) E / 2 Where the beam effectively stops (dose <10%).
For 12 MeV, range is 6cm.
5

Bolus, Modifiers & Normalization

5.1 Physics of Bolus

Material with density similar to tissue (water equivalent) placed on the skin.

EffectDescription
1. Eliminates Skin Sparing It brings Dmax closer to the skin surface.
Result: Increases skin dose (Erythema).
2. Compensates for Irregularity Fills in gaps (e.g., nose, ear) to make the surface flat for the beam.
3. Decreases Penetration The beam spends its energy in the bolus, so it doesn't go as deep into the patient.
Calculation: If you add 1cm of bolus to a 6MV beam (Dmax 1.5cm), the new Dmax is at 0.5cm depth in the patient (1.5 - 1.0).

5.2 Normalization

Definition: The process of defining where "100%" of the dose is.

  • Normalize to Max (Dmax): The hottest point in the plan is 100%. All other points are < 100%.
  • Normalize to Isocenter: The isocenter receives 100%. Points elsewhere might be hotter (e.g., 105% or 110%).

5.3 Wedge Pairs

  • Heel-to-Heel: Wedges are always placed "Heel to Heel" to compensate for the hot spot created by overlapping beams (like in a breast tangent or 90-degree plan).
