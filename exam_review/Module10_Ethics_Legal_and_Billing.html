ARRT Standards of Ethics

1.1 Code vs. Rules

Document Nature Example
Code of Ethics Aspirational (Goals). What we should do. "The radiologic technologist acts to advance the principal objective of the profession..."
Rules of Ethics Enforceable (Laws). What we must do. "Fraud or deceit in procuring certification." "Conviction of a crime."
Exam Tip: If the question asks what a therapist "strives" for, it's the Code. If it asks what leads to "sanctions" or "revocation," it's the Rules.
2

Legal Doctrines & Torts

2.1 Intentional Torts (Willful Acts)

  • Assault: The threat of touching in an injurious way. (Verbal threat: "If you move again, I will tie you down.")
  • Battery: Unlawful touching without consent. (e.g., Treating a patient against their will, or performing an exam on the wrong patient).
  • False Imprisonment: Restraining a patient without a physician's order or against their will. (e.g., Using velcro straps/immobilization merely for convenience, not safety).
  • Libel: Written defamation of character. (e.g., Writing insults in a patient's chart).
  • Slander: Spoken defamation of character. (e.g., Gossiping about a patient in the hallway).
  • Invasion of Privacy: Breach of confidentiality (HIPAA) or exposing a patient's body unnecessarily.

2.2 Unintentional Torts (Negligence)

  • Negligence: Failure to use such care as a "reasonably prudent person" would use under like circumstances.
  • Malpractice: Professional negligence. Bad practice that causes harm.
The 4 Elements of Malpractice: To be sued, 4 things must be proven: 1. Duty (You were assigned the patient). 2. Breach (You didn't follow the standard of care). 3. Injury (The patient was hurt). 4. Causation (Your breach caused the injury).

2.3 Legal Doctrines (Latin Terms)

Term Translation Meaning
Res Ipsa Loquitur "The thing speaks for itself" The injury could not have occurred without negligence. (e.g., Leaving a clamp inside a patient, or radiating the wrong body part).
Respondeat Superior "Let the master answer" The employer (Hospital) is liable for the employee's (Therapist's) actions while working.
Doctrine of Personal Liability "You are responsible" Each person is liable for his or her own negligent conduct. (Just because the doctor ordered it doesn't mean you are immune if you knew it was wrong).
Doctrine of Borrowed Servant "Captain of the Ship" A physician may be liable for wrongful acts committed by hospital employees under the physician's orders.

Billing Terminology

3.1 Charges Breakdown

  • Professional Component: Covers the physician's cognitive work (e.g., Contouring, Plan Review).
  • Technical Component: Covers the facility's costs (e.g., Machine time, Therapist labor).

3.2 Complexity Levels

Level Definition Example
Simple Single field or parallel opposed (POP). No blocks or simple blocks. Whole Brain, Spine (PA).
Intermediate 3 or more fields, or multiple blocks. 3-Field Plan.
Complex Highly custom. Rotation, wedges, custom blocking, contrast used. IMRT, VMAT, 4D-CT Sim.
