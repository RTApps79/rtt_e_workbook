Module 11: Final Test Strategy & Tactics

How to beat the test: Pacing, Logic, and Avoiding Traps.

1

The "Distractor" Trap

ARRT questions often include information that is medically true but irrelevant to the specific question asked.

Example: "A patient with a T2N1M0 lung tumor is treated with 6MV photons. The patient has a pacemaker. What is the Dmax depth?"

  • The Trap: You might think about the pacemaker dose limit (2 Gy) or the staging.
  • The Reality: The question only asks for Dmax depth of 6MV (1.5 cm). The tumor stage and pacemaker are distractors.
Tactic: Read the last sentence FIRST. "What is the Dmax depth?" Then look for data that answers THAT specific question.
2

The "Best" Answer (Safety First)

Sometimes two answers are correct, but one is "better" (more specific or safer).

Scenario: "A patient falls in the waiting room."

  • Option A: Help them up.
  • Option B: Call for a nurse/doctor.

Analysis: Option A is kind, but risky (they might have a fracture). Option B is legally safer and follows scope of practice.

Tactic: Always choose the answer that keeps the patient safest and keeps you within your legal scope of practice.
3

Math Management

  • The 2-Minute Rule: If a math problem takes >2 minutes, FLAG IT and move on.
  • Ballpark It: Before calculating, guess the range.
    Example: "If distance doubles, intensity should drop a lot (to 1/4)." If your calculator says the number got bigger, you know you made a keystroke error immediately.
4

Trust Your Gut

Research shows that changing answers usually leads to changing a Right answer to a Wrong one.

Final Rule: Only change an answer if you find definitive proof in the question text (like a word you misread: "EXCEPT" or "NOT") that proves your first choice was wrong.
