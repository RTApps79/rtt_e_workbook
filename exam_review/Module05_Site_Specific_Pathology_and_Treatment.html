Module 05: Comprehensive Site-Specific Pathology & Treatment

Detailed breakdown of Histopathology, Tumor Location, and Treatment specifics for all major sites.

CNS
Central Nervous System (Brain & Spinal Cord)
Most Common Histopathology Gliomas (Astrocytoma / Glioblastoma Multiforme)

Specifically, Glioblastoma Multiforme (GBM) is the most common and most aggressive primary adult brain tumor.

Note: Metastatic disease (from Lung/Breast) is more common than primary brain tumors.

Specific Tumor Locations Cerebrum (Adults) vs. Cerebellum (Children)
  • Adults: Supratentorial (Upper brain).
  • Children: Infratentorial (Posterior Fossa/Cerebellum) - e.g., Medulloblastoma.
Treatment Pearl (CSI): For Medulloblastoma, the entire subarachnoid space (Brain + Spine) must be treated because the tumor seeds via CSF (drop metastases).
