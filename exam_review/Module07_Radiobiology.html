The Cell: Structure & Function

1.1 Cell Organelles

[Image of human cell structure with organelles labeled]

OrganelleFunctionRadiobiology Significance
NucleusControl center; contains DNA/Chromosomes.The Master Target. Direct hits here cause cell death or mutation.
CytoplasmFluid (Protoplasm) outside the nucleus. Mostly water (80%).Site of Indirect Action (Radiolysis of water).
MitochondriaPowerhouse (Energy/ATP production).Damage here reduces cell function but is rarely fatal alone.
RibosomesProtein synthesis.Site of translation (mRNA -> Protein).
Lysosomes"Garbage disposal." Contains enzymes.If ruptured by radiation, enzymes leak and digest the cell (Autolysis).
Cell MembraneSelectively permeable barrier.Holds the cell together. High doses can rupture it.

Direct vs. Indirect Action

2.1 The Mechanisms

Action TypeProcessRadiation Type
Direct ActionRadiation hits the DNA nucleus directly.High LET (Neutrons, Alphas, Protons).
Indirect ActionRadiation hits water → free radicals → DNA damage.Low LET (X-rays, Gamma rays).
