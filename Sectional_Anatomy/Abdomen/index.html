Page 1: The Abdomen — Boundaries and Levels

IV and oral contrast sharpen organ and vessel borders on abdominal CT. Vertebral levels orient you: diaphragm ~T8–T10, GE junction ~T10–T11, pancreatic head ~L1–L2, kidneys ~T12–L3, and the adult cord ending at the conus medullaris ~L1–L2.

A quick orientation rule that holds on every axial slice: the aorta sits anterior/left of the spine, with the inferior vena cava immediately to its right.

Quick Check!

In which quadrant is the spleen located?

B. The spleen sits in the left upper quadrant, posterolateral to the stomach; the liver occupies the right upper quadrant.

Page 3: Peritoneal vs. Retroperitoneal

A core abdominal distinction is which organs sit within the peritoneal cavity versus behind it — it explains motion, spread patterns, and approach.

RetroperitonealPeritoneal
Kidneys & adrenals, uretersLiver, spleen, stomach
Pancreas (mostly), duodenum (2nd–4th parts)Gallbladder
Aorta & IVC; ascending/descending colonTransverse colon, jejunum/ileum

Quick Check!

Which of these is a retroperitoneal organ?

A. The kidneys (with adrenals, pancreas, duodenum, aorta, and IVC) are retroperitoneal; the spleen and gallbladder are intraperitoneal.

Quick Check!

Abdominal para-aortic nodes are described relative to which structure?

A. Para-aortic (and paracaval) nodal stations follow the aorta and IVC anterior to the spine — the reason those vessels are such important orientation landmarks.

Page 5: Anatomic Verification in the Abdomen

Abdominal targets combine respiratory motion with variable bowel/stomach filling, so verification is organ- and target-focused.

  • CBCT soft-tissue matching to the organ (liver dome, kidney) rather than bone alone.
  • Motion management: 4D-CT/ITV, breath-hold, gating, or abdominal compression.
  • Fiducial markers (liver, pancreas) provide a visible surrogate on daily imaging.

Quick Check!

Why are fiducial markers often used for liver or pancreas verification?

A. Low soft-tissue contrast plus respiratory/filling motion makes some abdominal targets hard to localize; fiducials give a clear, trackable surrogate.
