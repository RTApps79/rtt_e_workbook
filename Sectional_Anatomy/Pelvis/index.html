Sectional Anatomy: Pelvis

Interactive Lesson

Page 1: Imaging the Pelvis — Bony Framework

The bony pelvis is the reproducible scaffold for localization and matching. Each os coxae is three fused bones — ilium, ischium, pubis — meeting at the acetabulum.

The pubic symphysis, femoral heads, and sacral promontory make excellent bony references — but as the next pages show, for the prostate they only get you close.

Page 2: Bladder, Prostate, and Rectum

The central pelvic organs stack in a consistent front-to-back order that is the key to reading any pelvic slice.

Quick Check!

On axial pelvic CT, what is the front-to-back order of the central male pelvic organs?

A. Anterior-to-posterior: bladder (with prostate at its base inferiorly), then rectum posteriorly. This order holds on every slice and orients you instantly.

Because the bladder is the end of this pathway, how full it is at simulation and at each treatment directly changes its size and the position of neighboring organs — the theme of the next page.

Page 4: Filling, Motion, and Interfractional Change

The pelvis looks bony-stable but its soft-tissue targets move day to day.

  • Bladder filling changes bladder size and pushes the uterus/prostate; protocols standardize a comfortably full or empty bladder.
  • Rectal filling and gas displace the prostate/cervix anteriorly — a major source of motion relative to the pelvic bones.
  • This is why a bony match alone is inadequate for the prostate: the gland moves independently of the pelvis.

Quick Check!

Why is a soft-tissue or fiducial match preferred over a pure bony match for prostate treatment?

A. Rectal and bladder filling shift the prostate independently of the skeleton, so verification must localize the gland itself (CBCT soft tissue or implanted fiducials).

Page 5: Anatomic Verification in the Pelvis

The pelvis is the textbook example of choosing the right match:

  • Bony match where the target tracks the skeleton (many gynecologic nodal volumes, bone targets).
  • Soft-tissue / fiducial match for the prostate, which shifts with filling.
  • Bladder/rectum filling protocols and increasingly MR-guided workflows keep interfractional anatomy consistent with the plan.

Quick Check!

For prostate SBRT, which is the most reliable daily localization surrogate when soft-tissue contrast is limited?

A. Fiducials implanted in the prostate give a clear, trackable surrogate that moves with the gland — more reliable than distant bony landmarks when soft-tissue contrast is limited.

Page 6: Wrap-Up

You've read the bony pelvis and the bladder/prostate/rectum stack on real CT, explored the urinary tract in 3D, and seen why bladder and rectal filling force a deliberate choice between bony and soft-tissue (fiducial) matching.
