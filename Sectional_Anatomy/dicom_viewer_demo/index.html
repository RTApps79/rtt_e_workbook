    How to use

    • Click / drag any pane to move the crosshair.
    • Scroll wheel over a pane to page through its slices.
    • Orientation letters: Anterior, Posterior, Left, Right, Superior, Inferior (assumes Head-First Supine).

    Volumes are downsampled for the web; full-resolution planning scans have finer detail. Orientation follows the standard radiologic convention (patient's right on the viewer's left).

    Reading a CT: Hounsfield Units and windowing

    Every pixel is a Hounsfield Unit (HU), calibrated so water = 0 and air = −1000. Fat is around −100 to −50, muscle and soft tissue +20 to +80, and cortical bone +400 and up. The window level sets the brightness (the center HU) and the window width sets the contrast (the HU range spread from black to white). Try the presets and watch the same slice reveal lung detail, then soft tissue, then bone. This is the same HU data a treatment planning system converts to electron density for dose calculation.
