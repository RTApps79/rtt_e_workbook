            <div id="lesson-page-1" class="lesson-page active">

                <h3>Page 1: Introduction to ARRT Standards of Ethics</h3>

                <p>The Standards of Ethics of The American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) apply to all currently or formerly certified/registered individuals, and to applicants <span class="citation">[cite: 1]</span>. Radiologic Technology encompasses disciplines like radiography, radiation therapy, nuclear medicine, and more <span class="citation">[cite: 2]</span>.</p>

                <p><strong>Statement of Purpose:</strong> The primary purpose of these ethics requirements is to identify individuals who have internalized professional values that lead them to act in the best interests of patients <span class="citation">[cite: 4]</span>. These standards provide proactive guidance, promote ethical behavior[cite: 7], and support ARRT's mission of high standards in patient care by restricting those who exhibit inconsistent behavior[cite: 8].</p>

                <p>The Standards consist of two main parts: the Code of Ethics and the Rules of Ethics.</p>

            </div>

 

            <div id="lesson-page-2" class="lesson-page">

                <h3>Page 2: The ARRT Code of Ethics - Guiding Principles (Part 1)</h3>

                <p>The <span class="highlight">Code of Ethics</span> is an aspirational guide for professional conduct related to patients, employers, colleagues, and other healthcare team members <span class="citation">[cite: 10, 12]</span>. It aims to maintain high ethical conduct and ensure patient protection, safety, and comfort <span class="citation">[cite: 11]</span>.</p>

                <p><strong>Key Principles Include:</strong></p>

                <ul>

                    <li><strong>Principle 1:</strong> Act professionally, respond to patient needs, and support colleagues in providing quality care <span class="citation">[cite: 12]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 2:</strong> Advance the profession's objective to serve humanity with full respect for dignity <span class="citation">[cite: 13]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 3:</strong> Deliver care unrestricted by personal attributes or illness nature, and without discrimination <span class="citation">[cite: 14]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 4:</strong> Practice based on theoretical knowledge, use equipment appropriately, and employ proper procedures <span class="citation">[cite: 15]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 5:</strong> Assess situations, exercise care and judgment, assume responsibility, and act in the patient's best interest <span class="citation">[cite: 16, 17]</span>.</li>

                </ul>

                <h3>Page 3: The ARRT Code of Ethics - Guiding Principles (Part 2)</h3>

                <p>Continuing with the aspirational Code of Ethics:</p>

                <ul>

                    <li><strong>Principle 6:</strong> Act as an agent by observation and communication to obtain pertinent information for diagnosis/treatment, recognizing interpretation and diagnosis are outside the RTT scope <span class="citation">[cite: 19]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 7:</strong> Use equipment and procedures according to accepted standards, minimizing radiation exposure to all <span class="citation">[cite: 20]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 8:</strong> Practice ethically and protect the patient's right to quality care <span class="citation">[cite: 21]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 9:</strong> Respect patient confidences and privacy, revealing information only as required by law or to protect welfare <span class="citation">[cite: 22]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 10:</strong> Continually strive to improve knowledge and skills through CE, sharing knowledge, and investigating new practices <span class="citation">[cite: 23]</span>.</li>

                    <li><strong>Principle 11:</strong> Refrain from using illegal drugs or controlled substances that impair professional judgment or ability to practice safely <span class="citation">[cite: 24]</span>.</li>

                </ul>

            <div id="lesson-page-4" class="lesson-page">

                <h3>Page 4: Introduction to the ARRT Rules of Ethics</h3>

                <p>The <span class="highlight">Rules of Ethics</span> are the second part of the Standards. Unlike the Code, these are <span class="highlight">mandatory and enforceable</span> standards of minimally acceptable professional conduct for all RTTs and Candidates <span class="citation">[cite: 25, 26]</span>. They are intended to promote the protection, safety, and comfort of patients <span class="citation">[cite: 28]</span>.</p>

                <p><strong>Key Points:</strong></p>

                <ul>

                    <li><strong>Enforceable:</strong> Violations are subject to sanctions[cite: 30].</li>

                    <li><strong>Duty to Report:</strong> Registered Technologists must notify ARRT of any ethics violations (their own or others'), including state licensing issues and criminal charges/convictions, within 30 days or at annual renewal, whichever is first <span class="citation">[cite: 30, 91]</span>. Applicants must report within 30 days of occurrence <span class="citation">[cite: 31]</span>.</li>

                </ul>

                <h3>Page 5: Highlights from the Rules of Ethics - Part 1</h3>

                <p>Violations subject RTTs/Candidates to sanctions. Categories include:</p>

                <div class="key-principle"><strong>1. Fraud or Deceptive Practices:</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li>In procuring/maintaining certification, employment, or state licenses (e.g., altering documents, falsely claiming ARRT registration) <span class="citation">[cite: 34, 35]</span>.</li>

                        <li>False/misleading communications about education, credentials, or experience <span class="citation">[cite: 36]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Fraudulent billing practices (e.g., Medicare/Medicaid fraud) <span class="citation">[cite: 37]</span>.</li>

                    </ul>

                </div>

                <div class="key-principle"><strong>2. Subversion:</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li>Subverting ARRT exams, CQR (including SSAs), or Education Requirements <span class="citation">[cite: 39, 53]</span>. This includes disclosing exam/SSA info, soliciting/receiving exam/SSA info, copying/transmitting materials, or providing false CE/clinical info <span class="citation">[cite: 41, 43, 44, 55]</span>.</li>

                         <li>Failure to cooperate with ARRT investigations, including making false statements <span class="citation">[cite: 59, 60, 61]</span>.</li>

                    </ul>

                </div>

                <h3>Page 6: Highlights from the Rules of Ethics - Part 2</h3>

                <div class="key-principle"><strong>3. Unprofessional Conduct:</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li>Failure to conform to minimal acceptable standards or governmental rules regarding practice; or practice that may create unnecessary danger <span class="citation">[cite: 63, 64, 65]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Sexual misconduct with a patient or sexually demeaning behavior <span class="citation">[cite: 67, 68]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Unethical conduct likely to deceive, defraud, or harm the public or patient <span class="citation">[cite: 70, 71]</span>.</li>

                    </ul>

                </div>

                 <div class="key-principle"><strong>4. Scope of Practice Violations:</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li>Performing procedures one is not competent to perform <span class="citation">[cite: 72]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Improper delegation or acceptance of functions that could endanger a patient <span class="citation">[cite: 75]</span>.</li>

                    </ul>

                </div>

                 <div class="key-principle"><strong>5. Fitness to Practice:</strong> Inability to practice with reasonable skill/safety due to illness, alcohol/drugs, or mental/physical condition <span class="citation">[cite: 76, 77]</span>.</div>

                 <div class="key-principle"><strong>6. Improper Management of Patient Records:</strong> False entries or failing to protect confidential patient information <span class="citation">[cite: 79, 81]</span>.</div>

                <h3>Page 7: Highlights from the Rules of Ethics - Part 3 & Duty to Report</h3>

                 <div class="key-principle"><strong>7. Violation of State/Federal Law or Regulatory Rule:</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li>Violating narcotics or controlled substance laws <span class="citation">[cite: 83]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Violating a rule from a state/federal authority or certification board leading to licensure/certification action, or failing to report such actions to ARRT <span class="citation">[cite: 84]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Criminal proceedings (convictions, guilt findings even if adjudication withheld, certain pleas, sex offender registration) <span class="citation">[cite: 85, 86, 87, 88, 90]</span>.</li>

                    </ul>

                </div>

                <div class="key-principle"><strong>8. Duty to Report:</strong>

                    <ul>

                        <li>Knowing of a violation by any RTT or Candidate and failing to promptly report it in writing to ARRT <span class="citation">[cite: 91]</span>.</li>

                        <li>Failing to <span class="highlight">immediately report to one's supervisor</span> an error made in connection with patient care (imaging, treating, or caring) that could be potentially harmful, unethical, or improper. This duty exists whether or not actual injury occurred <span class="citation">[cite: 92, 93, 94, 95]</span>.</li>

                    </ul>
