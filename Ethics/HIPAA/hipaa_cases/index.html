Activity: HIPAA Case Study Analysis

Before You Begin...

Purpose

This activity challenges you to apply your knowledge of HIPAA's Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification rules by analyzing complex, realistic scenarios. It aims to strengthen your critical thinking and decision-making skills in real-world situations.

Task

You will read two detailed case studies relevant to radiation therapy. For each case, reflect on the situation and use the provided text areas to type your answers to the prompt questions. There is no automatic scoring; this is an exercise in thoughtful analysis and application.

Criteria for Success

A successful completion involves providing thorough, well-reasoned answers that:

  • Accurately identify potential HIPAA issues.
  • Reference specific HIPAA rules where applicable.
  • Propose ethically and legally sound courses of action.
You can earn a printable "Certificate of Completion" after engaging with the cases.

Case Study 1: The 'Helpful' Colleague

An RTT, Tom, is covering a shift and needs to access patient setup notes. His own EMR login isn't working. His colleague, Sarah, is busy but quickly tells Tom her password so he can log in as her to get the information and keep the schedule moving. Later, Tom uses Sarah's login to quickly check the appointment time for his uncle, who is being treated in another department.

1. Identify all potential HIPAA violations in this scenario.
2. Which specific HIPAA Rules (Privacy/Security) and Safeguards (Admin, Physical, Tech) are relevant?
3. What should Tom and Sarah have done differently? What actions should be taken now?

Case Study 2: The Lost USB Drive

An RTT, Lisa, is working on a presentation about a new treatment technique. To work on it at home, she downloads several patient treatment plans (including names, IDs, and plan details) onto a personal, unencrypted USB drive. The next day, she realizes the USB drive is missing. She thinks she might have left it at a coffee shop.

1. Does this situation constitute a HIPAA breach? Why or why not?
2. What are Lisa's immediate responsibilities? What are the facility's responsibilities under the Breach Notification Rule?
3. What Security Rule safeguards could have prevented this situation?
