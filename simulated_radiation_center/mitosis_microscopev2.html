Pathology Viewer

Your Mission: Pathologist

Examine the tissue sample. /* cite: 41 */ If mitosis is active, observe cell division.

  • Use controls to adjust Focus, Stage (X, Y), and Zoom.
  • Normal Cells: Divide slowly.
  • Benign Tumor Cells: Do not divide in this simulation.
  • Malignant Cells: Divide rapidly, contributing to tumor growth. /* cite: 41, 42 */ Note their irregular shapes.
  • Rapidly dividing cells are generally more sensitive to radiation (Law of Bergonie and Tribondeau).
    • Law of Bergonie and Tribondeau: This law states that cells are more sensitive to radiation if they are immature, undifferentiated, and rapidly dividing. Malignant cells, particularly high-grade ones, exhibit these characteristics, making them prime targets for radiation therapy.
  • Apoptosis (Programmed Cell Death): Normal cells have a built-in mechanism for self-destruction when they are damaged or no longer needed. Malignant cells often lose this crucial ability, leading to uncontrolled growth and tumor formation.
