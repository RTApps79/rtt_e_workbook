How each technique shapes the dose

POP (parallel-opposed pair) — two beams 180° apart give a simple, uniform dose corridor but expose everything along that axis. 3-Field and 4-Field box concentrate high dose where the beams overlap at the target, keeping entrance dose spread out. Arc (non-VMAT) rotates an unmodulated beam, smearing entrance dose into a ring and tightening the high-dose region around isocenter. IMRT uses several fixed, modulated fields whose fluence is carved to avoid the OAR — producing a concave high-dose region that wraps around it. VMAT combines modulation with continuous rotation for conformal dose delivered in one or two arcs. A dynamic wedge attenuates one side of a field, tilting the isodose lines — used to compensate for sloping surfaces or to shape a wedged-pair dose. This is an illustrative teaching model, not a clinical dose calculation.
