Page 1: The Vertebral Body Is Your Roadmap

In head and neck radiation therapy, the cervical vertebral bodies are your most reliable landmark — field borders and contouring decisions are constantly described in relation to the C-spine. This lesson connects the key anatomical structures you need to treat (or spare) to their corresponding vertebral level, exactly the skill you'll need reading any H&N planning CT.

Page 2: C1–C2 — The Nasopharynx and Oropharynx

LevelStructure / Event
C1Nasopharynx (the uppermost pharynx). The Node of Rouvière, a key retropharyngeal lymph node, sits anterior to C1–C2.
C2Oropharynx begins here, extending down to C3.

Page 3: C3 — Where Three Structures Begin

C3 is a genuinely important landmark: the epiglottis sits here, marking the superior border of both the larynx and the hypopharynx — both structures begin at this level.

Quick Check!

Which vertebral level marks the epiglottis and the start of both the larynx and hypopharynx?

B. C3 is where the epiglottis sits, marking the superior border of both the larynx and hypopharynx.

Page 4: C4–C5 — The Glottis

The true vocal cords (glottis) — the main functional body of the larynx — sit at roughly C4–C5. The pyriform sinuses of the hypopharynx lie lateral to the larynx at this same level, continuing posteriorly alongside it.

Page 5: C6 — Where Three Structures End (or Begin)

At C6: the larynx ends at the inferior border of the cricoid cartilage; the hypopharynx also ends here, emptying into the esophagus, which begins just after the cricoid cartilage at this same level.

Quick Check!

At C6, which cartilage marks the end of the larynx?

B. The cricoid cartilage's inferior border marks the end of the larynx at C6, right where the esophagus begins.
