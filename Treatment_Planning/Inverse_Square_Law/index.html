Why this matters in the clinic

The inverse square law governs any point-like radiation source: it is why an extended-SSD treatment delivers less dose per monitor unit than at standard distance (and needs an inverse-square correction), why brachytherapy dose falls off so sharply around a seed, and part of why PDD increases with SSD. In the Beam & Dose Simulator, the exponential attenuation you see is layered on top of this geometric falloff. Note the law assumes a point source in a non-attenuating medium — in tissue, attenuation and scatter modify the pure 1/d² behavior.
