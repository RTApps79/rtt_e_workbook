What am I looking at? (the physics)

Build-up region. A megavoltage photon beam does not deposit its maximum dose at the skin. Photons first set electrons in motion, and those electrons travel a short distance before depositing energy. Dose therefore builds up from a relatively low surface (skin) dose to a peak at dmax. Higher energy → deeper dmax and lower skin dose (this is the skin-sparing effect).

Attenuation. Beyond dmax, dose falls off roughly exponentially as the beam is attenuated by tissue, until it exits the far side. Higher energy beams are more penetrating (they fall off more slowly).

Why tissue matters — the sectional-anatomy bridge. Attenuation depends on electron density, not distance alone. The simulator converts each pixel's Hounsfield value to relative electron density, so the beam accumulates "water-equivalent depth" as it travels. Through lung the beam accrues depth slowly and stays hotter deeper; through bone it attenuates faster. Rotate the gantry so the beam passes through a lung versus through the vertebral body and watch the curve change.

Model parameters are typical teaching values; dmax and penetration vary with field size, SSD, and machine calibration in reality.
