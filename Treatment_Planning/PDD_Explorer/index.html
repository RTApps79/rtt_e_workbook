Percentage Depth Dose (PDD)

PDD is the dose at a given depth expressed as a percentage of the dose at dmax, along the central axis of an SSD setup. It rises to 100% at dmax then falls with depth — and that fall-off depends on energy, field size, and SSD. Change each and watch the curve respond.

The beam enters at the skin surface (top) and travels down into the patient. Dose builds to dmax just beneath the surface, then falls with depth. Higher energy, larger field, and longer SSD all raise the PDD at depth.

What changes the PDD, and why

Energy ↑ → more penetrating, higher PDD at depth and deeper dmax. Field size ↑ → more scattered dose reaching depth, higher PDD. SSD ↑ → higher PDD at depth, because the inverse-square fall-off across the patient is gentler when the source is farther away (the inverse square law again). PDD is tied to an SSD setup; for isocentric (SAD) setups we use TMR instead, which removes the distance dependence.
