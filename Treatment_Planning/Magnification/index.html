Magnification

A diverging X-ray beam casts an enlarged shadow of anything it passes through — the closer an object is to the source, the bigger its image. This geometry sets how anatomy appears on a simulator radiograph, DRR, or port film, and it is the same divergence behind the inverse square law.

Why this matters in the clinic

On a simulator film or DRR, structures nearer the X-ray source are magnified more than structures near the detector, so a measured size on the image is larger than the true anatomic size. Understanding magnification lets you back-calculate true dimensions and interpret field sizes correctly. The same divergence that magnifies an image is what enlarges a treatment field at extended SSD and what spreads photons in the inverse square law — three views of one geometry.
