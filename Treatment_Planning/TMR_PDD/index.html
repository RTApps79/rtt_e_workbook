Tissue-Maximum Ratio (TMR)

PDD is measured with a fixed SSD setup, so it carries the beam's inverse-square fall-off with distance. TMR is measured at a fixed distance to the point (an isocentric / SAD setup), which removes that distance dependence — so TMR is essentially independent of SSD. The two are linked by a single inverse-square factor.

PDD vs. TMR: which setup, and why it matters

Use PDD for fixed-SSD treatments (patient surface at a set distance, e.g. 100 cm). Use TMR for isocentric (SAD) treatments, where the tumor sits at the isocenter and the gantry rotates around it — the distance to the calculation point never changes, so TMR depends on depth and field size but not SSD. That independence is exactly why isocentric planning is convenient for multi-field and arc treatments. The bracket converting one to the other is the same inverse square geometry seen throughout these tools.
